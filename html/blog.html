The power of being humble


4/9/2017

As I have finished the mentorship stage of my education, this is a discipline I want to excel at my entire career, being humble.

I have struggled with this in my current career as a newer firefighter, and it has been a hard lesson to swallow at times, granted I have learned to be much better at this skill in my current profession. That being said, I think of the junior firefighters that come in to the department, and the ones I respect the most. When they are willing to listen intently, to a veteran firefighter with a lot of years of experience, who want to try to teach them something, that carries huge respect and weight.

I have tried to be that kind of humble in my learning and now as I enter the mentorship/education part of school, I am dedicated to being that type of new guy even more. In my education I have found that this community is extremely willing to share their knowledge with new developers/students like myself. I have learned so much from forums, my instructor, web communities, and even my fellow students. I have also learned that being humble can, and will in my experience, bring you very far in this profession. I have learned that as coders we love to share our new knowledge, and are a very open community. This has made me love the struggles and accomplishments along the way. That being said, I have been willing to listen to anyone that wants to share coding knowledge with me, and have looked to experience developers for guidance.

So I know this might seem like an obvious skill, but has been difficult for me at times. I feel that with this profession, and all of the wonderful people I have meet along the way, this is a skill that will be easy to hone. So as this profession always has new technologies emerging, and continual learning required, I am ready to be even more humble. The future of this profession is going to be an exciting one, and I am excited to be a part of it.
